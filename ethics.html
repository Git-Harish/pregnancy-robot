Ethical and Societal Implications
Reproductive Rights

Concerns about further restrictions on abortion access as fetal visibility increases.

Devaluation of Pregnancy

Risk of reducing pregnancy to a mechanical process, potentially diminishing the role of women.

Legal Status

Unclear legal frameworks for children born via artificial wombs and pregnancy robots.

Access Inequality

Despite lower costs than surrogacy, technology may still create reproductive disparities.

"We have held discussion forums with authorities in Guangdong Province and submitted related proposals while discussing policy and legislation." - Dr. Zhang Qifeng

The "fetal container model" promoted by this technology risks viewing pregnant women as merely "uterine environments" or "natural incubators."
