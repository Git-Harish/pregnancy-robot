The Concept of Artificial Wombs (Ectogenesis)

Ectogenesis

The growth of an organism outside its natural biological environment, specifically the development of a fetus outside the uterus.

Historical Development

Research on artificial wombs began with partial ectogenesis for premature infants, evolving toward full gestation support systems.

Key Components

Artificial amniotic fluid, nutrient delivery system (mimicking placenta), and monitoring technology for fetal development.

Integration with Robotics

The pregnancy robot concept combines artificial womb technology with humanoid robotics to create a mobile gestational environment.
